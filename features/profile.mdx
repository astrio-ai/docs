## Your profile

Every user has a unique username on Astrio. That username identifies your profile on the platform and appears in your public profile URL, so people can recognize you and find your work in a consistent place.

Your profile is publicly visible and shareable: anyone with your profile link can open it. Use that when you want to show off live or in-progress sites, send someone straight to your portfolio of work, or point teammates to a single page that lists what you own and what you have saved.

From your own account, the same page is your shortcut to that inventory: one view of owned and saved projects without hunting through the rest of Astrio.

## What appears on your profile

- Projects you own — Every project where you are the owner is listed here, so you can scan what you have shipped or are still building without hunting through the rest of the product.
- Saved projects — Projects you have bookmarked or saved for later also show up alongside owned projects, so drafts, experiments, and references you filed away stay easy to find.

Together, owned and saved projects give you a practical overview: your live and in-progress sites, plus anything you wanted to keep at hand.

## How to use it

Use your profile when you want a quick inventory of everything under your account, jump back into a project, or remind yourself which items you saved while exploring Astrio. It is meant to reduce tab-hopping and keep your workspace understandable as the list of sites grows.
