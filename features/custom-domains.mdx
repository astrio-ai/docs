## Supported Domain Types

### Root Domains
Connect your main domain (e.g., `yourcompany.com`) directly to your Astrio project.

### Subdomains
Use subdomains like `app.yourcompany.com` or `demo.yourcompany.com` for different projects or environments.

### Multiple Domains
Connect multiple domains to the same project or use different domains for different projects.

Astrio does **not** sell domains. You connect a **domain or subdomain you already control** and verify it with DNS.

## Prerequisites

1. You have owner or editor permissions to connect and disconnect domains.
2. **[Publish your project](/features/publish)** first. Custom domain setup is available after the project is published.
3. You have access to the DNS settings for the domain or subdomain you want to connect. Ownership verification requires adding the DNS CNAME records Astrio provides you during setup.

## Connect your domain

Follow these steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a custom domain">
    After you publish, open the **Publish** modal. Use **Add custom domain** to open the **Domain** tab in the project **Settings** panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your domain">
    Enter your domain or subdomain (for example, `yourdomain.com` or `blog.yourdomain.com`). Click **Request certificate** so Astrio can start validation and show the DNS records you need.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure DNS records">
    Astrio displays the DNS records you must add. They are **unique to your project**—always copy the **Name** and **Value** from the app, not from examples. In general:

    | Field | What it is |
    | ----- | ---------- |
    | **Type** | Usually `CNAME` |
    | **Name** | The **hostname** Astrio gives you to create at your DNS provider (copy it exactly, including any leading underscore or trailing dot) |
    | **Value** | The **target** Astrio gives you (often a long hostname used for certificate validation—copy it exactly) |

    In your DNS provider (for example Cloudflare, Route 53, or your registrar), create the CNAME exactly as shown.

    <Warning>
      If your provider supports a **proxy** (orange cloud in Cloudflare, etc.), **turn it off** for this validation record. Proxied records can block certificate validation.
    </Warning>

    Verification often completes in **about 1–15 minutes**, but DNS can take **up to 24–72 hours** to propagate everywhere.

    Use **Refresh status** in Astrio to re-check. When records are detected, click **Check DNS & activate**.

    Wait until the domain status is **Live**. Once the CNAME points correctly, Astrio can **issue SSL certificates** for your domain.

    You can **remove the custom domain** later from the same settings if you need to disconnect it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Verify DNS

Use a DNS lookup tool such as [dnschecker.org](https://dnschecker.org) to confirm your name resolves to the records Astrio expects. Large TTLs or caching can delay what you see locally; changes can take up to 72 hours in rare cases.
